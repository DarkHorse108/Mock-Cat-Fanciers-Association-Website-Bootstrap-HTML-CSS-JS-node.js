Welcome

The Cat Fancier's Association (CFA) International is the world's leading body in feline pedigree standardization, showmanship, and care. Click here to find a show near you.

Our Mission

The Association shall be a not-for-profit organization. Its objects shall be as follows: the welfare of all cats; the promotion and improvement of CFA recognized breeds of cats; the registering, recording or identifying by number or by other means the names and/or pedigrees of cats and kittens; the promulgation of rules for the management of cat shows; the licensing of cat shows held under the rules of this organization; and the promotion of the interests of breeders and exhibitors of pedigreed cats. - CFA Constitution Article II. source

History

The Cat Fancier's Association was founded as a non-profit organization in 1906, and was formerly part of the American Cat Association. The first official CFA show was held in Buffalo, New York, and the organization's first official Annual Business Meeting began shortly after in 1907 at Madison Square Garden.

Since that time, the organization has grown to over 650 clubs worldwide, ranging from the United States to China. Each year's Annual Championship Class International Cat Show brings thousands of feline enthusiasts, owners, breeders, and veterinary medical professionals from across the globe.
